Infrastructure Planning · Environmental Impact Assessment

Habitat Risk Assessment for Agricultural Infrastructure

Based on PhD thesis research — Merops philippinus, Gangetic Delta, India (2022)
01
The Challenge

Agricultural land loss in the Gangetic Delta has driven demand for river-cliff concretisation. This infrastructure development directly destroys the nesting habitats of Merops philippinus — a bird crucial for natural pest control in local croplands — threatening the ecosystem services that smallholder farmers depend upon.

Computational Methodology

Engineered a hybrid analytical pipeline combining process-based and machine-learning models. Integrated climate, soil, entomological, and aquatic predictors with birds' nesting rates, breeding success, and demographic variables as latent and response variables to identify optimal habitat patch locations.

Applied Impact

Delivered a data-driven spatial plan identifying exactly where habitat corridors must be preserved during concretisation. The model provides a blueprint for Environmental Impact Assessments (EIAs), balancing critical agricultural infrastructure with the conservation of ecosystem services — applicable to policy and land-use planning authorities.

Wildlife Management · Decision-Support Tool

Interactive Decision-Support for Ungulate Management

Based on the Forage Energy Database — IIASA postdoctoral research
02
The Challenge

Wildlife managers and conservationists require accessible, quantitative tools to calculate the carrying capacity and dietary energy requirements of boreal ungulate populations — information that is otherwise scattered across hundreds of primary literature sources in non-actionable formats.

Computational Methodology

Architected and deployed the Forage Energy Database — a dynamic web application synthesising Menke energy coefficients with Jacobs' D preference indices to compute preference-weighted metabolizable energy (ep) for plant species consumed by moose, roe deer, and red deer across all seasons.

Applied Impact

Transformed 72 static nutritional records into an interactive dashboard allowing stakeholders to filter by species and season, providing immediate, actionable metrics for habitat management, supplementary feeding strategies, and population energy-budget parameterisation.

Eco-Epidemiology · Endangered Species Conservation

Eco-Epidemiological Modelling for Endangered Wild Dogs

Based on: Reja et al. (2022) — SN Applied Sciences
03
The Challenge

Canine Distemper Virus outbreaks pose catastrophic extinction risks to fragmented populations of endangered wild dog species. Without quantitative transmission models, conservation authorities lack a rigorous basis for deploying targeted interventions in time-critical disease events.

Computational Methodology

Developed advanced ODE-based mathematical models to investigate disease transmission dynamics within vulnerable wildlife populations, incorporating demographic structure, disease progression stages, and population connectivity parameters calibrated to empirical field data.

Applied Impact

Formulated quantitative control strategies from model outputs, providing conservation authorities with a rigorous mathematical framework to deploy targeted vaccination and quarantine interventions and quantify their impact on extinction probability — directly informing IUCN threat assessment methodology.

Climate Change · Biodiversity Forecasting

Climate-Driven Species Distribution Forecasting for Scandinavia

Based on IIASA postdoctoral research — Taiga-Climate App (in communication)
04
The Challenge

Scandinavian game managers and conservation planners need accessible, scenario-based forecasts of how CMIP6 climate pathways will reshape habitat suitability for moose, roe deer, and grey wolf — the three keystone species of the boreal zone — across projection years 2025–2100.

Computational Methodology

Built hierarchical and stacked machine-learning Species Distribution Models integrating climate, vegetation, and land-cover predictors with CMIP6 SSP scenarios (SSP1–5). Integrated wolf–ungulate predator–prey ODE systems for trophic interaction layers within the distributional forecasts.

Applied Impact

Delivered the interactive Taiga-Climate web application — heatmaps of species density under each climate scenario, accessible to non-specialist game hunters, conservation planners, and policymakers via a public web interface without requiring technical expertise.
